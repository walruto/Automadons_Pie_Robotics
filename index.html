SUBSYSTEM DEEP DIVE

FORKLIFT / ELEVATOR MECHANISM

⬆ Elevator Rail

Chain-driven vertical rail system — carries the forklift carriage from ground level to max scoring height with sprockets top and bottom.

🍴 Forklift Forks

Two parallel forks scoop under the passenger. A servo-controlled latch retains it during transit and releases on dump.

💡 edward u tuff boi — lock in!

This subsystem is the key differentiator — while other teams stay low, we access the Bar Train high stack for bonus points.

WIRING + COMPONENT PLACEMENT

ELECTRICAL LAYOUT + SAFETY

🍓 RASPBERRY PI
  • ↳ Center-mounted (balance)
  • ↳ Away from motors (interference)
  • ↳ Elevated (keeps cool)
  • ↳ Top layer (debris protection)
  • ↳ Adhesive mounts
⚡ MOTOR / SERVO CTL
  • ↳ Near motors (short wires)
  • ↳ Edge placement
  • ↳ Reduces voltage drop
  • ↳ Servo on robot arm
🔋 PDB
  • ↳ Close to battery
  • ↳ Short high-current wires
  • ↳ Isolated from chassis
🛡 SAFETY / ORGANIZATION
  • ↳ Zip ties — wires away from gears
  • ↳ Heat shrink on all joints
  • ↳ Spiral wrap bundles
  • ↳ Labelled every wire
  • ↳ Pre-match wiring check ✅

⚠ RISK MITIGATION
Risk 1: Elevator stuck/chain slips
Score using tray without lifting; push passengers to zone; fix elevator later
Risk 2: Passenger won't fall out
Smoother tray surface OR add small "kicker" servo to eject passenger
Risk 3: Gate/latch breaks
Backup latch + hard stop in tray to retain passenger mechanically
Risk 4: Wiring comes loose
Zip-tie + label all wires; pre-match wiring check every match
Risk 5: Code not ready
Manual controls first; add automation incrementally
